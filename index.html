WHAT DOES A CAR REALLY COST?

by Christopher Lee, Ravi Sharma, Curran Daly, Jerry Hu and Peter Veerman


Case Study A

This is Bob. Bob is a recent graduate, and wants to buy a car in order to commute to his new job. Bob wants to buy a Jeep Wrangler for what he thinks will cost him $25,000. However, he is unaware of all the other costs that are involved in buying a car.

Loans

Because Bob is an early graduate of college, he does not have enough money to pay in full for the Jeep up front. Instead, he takes out loans and pays for the car over time.

Credit Score

Bob has not established a strong credit score, which indicates to the lender that Bob might not make his payments on time and in full. This is why he has a relatively high interest rate of 15%.

Down Payment

Bob does not have a lot of cash on hand, so he is able to only pay a $5,000 down payment, financing the remaining $20,000. Because he did not put that much down as a down payment, he will have to pay more interest over a longer period of time compared to if he put a $10,000 down payment.

Insurance

He has to pay more in insurance fees because of his teenage status, as teenagers are in more risk of getting into car accidents and engaging in risky driving.

Fuel Economy

In the long run, the poor fuel economy of the Jeep will incur a lot of costs that are hidden at the moment.

Maintenance

The Jeep is notorious for its unreliability, so Bob will have to take into account the large amount of maintenance and repair fees that are going to accumulate as the car gets older.

Depreciation

As with any other car, the moment Bob steps into his new car, the value of the Jeep depreciates. By the end of the first year, the value of the car would have depreciated by 20%.

Case Study B

Joe is middle aged, and is much more knowledgeable about financing a car and what costs go into buying a car. Joe chooses to buy a Honda Accord EX-L Sedan, which has an MSRP of $29,670, around $5,000 more than Bob's Jeep.

Credit Score

Joe still has to take out a loan, but his credit score is excellent, thereby getting him a low interest rate of 3% on his loan.

Down Payment

Joe also put down a larger down payment of $20,000. As a result, his loan term is shorter and he won't have to pay as much total interest over the duration of the loan.

Insurance

Because Joe is older, the auto insurance companies see him as more responsible and less likely to partake in risky driving behavior. Thus, his auto insurance costs are lower.

Fuel Economy

The Accords fuel efficiency is better than most other cars, helping him to save more money on fuel in the long run.
